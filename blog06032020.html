Weekly Report 06 March 2020

Published: 07/03/2020

Following one of the worst weeks for the stock market in years, the markets opened on Monday with a rebound. This was pushed by rate cuts by the Fed in the US and promises for action from the ECB. However, this rebound was short lived, losing its momentum by Tuesday, and once again leaving investors panicked about the nature of CoViD-19. By Thursday, the number of cases began to increase faster in the UK and US, causing the markets to swiftly downturn and begin wiping off some of the gains from earlier in the week. As of market close on Friday, American markets were marginally up for the week, leaving cash heavy investors with another great chance to buy into the market at a discount from previous highs. It’s still hard to ascertain how much damage CoViD-19 will damage the global economy, but it’s fairly certain that the next quarter will be significantly down. As an investor I am more worried about the humanitarian impact of the disease right now, rather than the economic impact.
