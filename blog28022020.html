Weekly Report 28 February 2020

Published: 29/02/2020

After a mixed week last week, the markets opened on Monday significantly lower due to fears about SARS-CoV-2. Investors are beginning to come to terms with the economic impact of the virus on the Chinese economy, meanwhile fears increased in Europe as Italy began measures to reduce the spread of the virus. This downward trend continued throughout the week, as fears began to take a global stage, with the WHO claiming the world is “not prepared” for the potential pandemic. This fear lead to the quickest S&P 500 correction (10% drop) in history, and as of the writing of this article, the S&P is 14.5% down from its previous high. It can be very easy to fall into the trap of fearing the movement of the market like many other traders and investors, so I’ll leave you with Warren Buffett’s quote of “we simply attempt to be fearful when others are greedy and to be greedy only when others are fearful.”
